The recorded forest area in the State occupies only 20182 sq. km i.e 19.95 % of its 222235 sq. km geographical area.  As per the National Forest Policy , 1988 , hilly regions like that of Jammu & Kashmir should aim at a forest cover of 66 % against the prescription of 33 % suggested in NFP for the plain regions of the country . Further , about 46 % of the reported cover is between crown density of 10- 40 % , leaving only about 11000 sq km of good forest with crown density of more than 40 %.

 

Due to excessive biotic pressure , heavy exploitation for purpose of timber, fuelwod  extraction , grazing and other local uses , the forest cover has been reduced and many areas are degraded .
