# NWIA Jammu and Kashmir Atlas Integration

## Source Information

**Title:** NWIA Jammu and Kashmir Atlas  
**Publisher:** Space Applications Centre (SAC), ISRO  
**Collaborator:** University of Kashmir  
**Publication Date:** February 2010  
**URL:** https://indianwetlands.in/uploads/NWIA_Jammu_and_Kashmir_Atlas.pdf  

## Geographic Scope

This integration uses the NWIA Atlas **exclusively for Kashmir Valley** wetland and hydrological intelligence.

### Covered Districts
- Kupwara
- Baramulla
- Srinagar
- Budgam
- Pulwama
- Anantnag
- Ganderbal
- Bandipora

### NOT Included
- Jammu region wetlands (except where directly relevant to Kashmir hydrology)
- Ladakh region (except as part of greater Indus Basin context)
- Other Indian states

## NWIA Wetland Classification System

The NWIA Atlas uses a numeric classification system for wetland types. The following codes are used in the Kashmir EcoWatch platform:

| Code | Name | Description | Kashmir Examples |
|------|------|-------------|------------------|
| 1101 | Lakes/Ponds | Natural inland lakes and ponds with permanent water spread | Wular Lake, Dal Lake, Manasbal Lake, Anchar Lake |
| 1103 | High Altitude Wetlands | Glacial origin lakes and wetlands above 3000m elevation | Tarsar, Marsar, Gangabal, Krishansar, Vishansar |
| 1104 | Riverine Wetlands | Marshy areas and floodplain wetlands along river systems | Hokersar, Shallabugh, Jhelum floodplain wetlands |
| 1106 | River/Stream | Flowing water bodies including major rivers and tributaries | Jhelum, Kishanganga, Lidder, Sind rivers |
| 1202 | Tanks/Ponds | Man-made water bodies for sediment trapping and water management | Dal Lake sediment trap, urban stormwater ponds |

## Major Lakes Reference

### Wular Lake
- **District:** Baramulla
- **NWIA Code:** 1101
- **Area:** 11,273 hectares (69% of Baramulla wetland area)
- **Elevation:** 1,575m
- **Significance:** Largest freshwater lake in South Asia, Ramsar site
- **Characteristics:** Significant seasonal variation, high turbidity (pre-monsoon), low turbidity (post-monsoon)

### Dal Lake
- **District:** Srinagar
- **NWIA Code:** 1101
- **Area:** 2,194 hectares
- **Elevation:** 1,583m
- **Significance:** Iconic urban lake, tourism hub
- **Characteristics:** Houseboats, shikaras, floating gardens, aquatic vegetation (6,254 ha district total)

### Manasbal Lake
- **District:** Ganderbal
- **NWIA Code:** 1101
- **Elevation:** 1,583m
- **Significance:** Deepest lake in Kashmir Valley
- **Characteristics:** Lotus cultivation, Mughal gardens, excellent water clarity

### Hokersar
- **District:** Srinagar
- **NWIA Code:** 1104
- **Elevation:** 1,585m
- **Significance:** Ramsar site, most important for migratory waterfowl
- **Characteristics:** Central Asian Flyway stopover, 100,000+ wintering birds

## River Systems

### Jhelum River
- **NWIA Code:** 1106
- **Length in Valley:** 177 km
- **Districts:** All Kashmir Valley districts
- **Significance:** Principal river, lifeline of Kashmir
- **Characteristics:** Rises from Verinag spring, navigable from Khannabal to Wular, dissects Srinagar

### Major Tributaries
- **Kishanganga:** Kupwara, Bandipora
- **Lidder:** Anantnag, Pulwama (originates from Kolahoi Glacier)
- **Sind:** Ganderbal, Srinagar (originates from Machoi Glacier)

## Hydrological Characteristics (NWIA Observations)

### Water Spread Variation
- **Lakes:** Significant seasonal fluctuation in open water area
- **Rivers:** Slight variation due to glacier melt

### Aquatic Vegetation
- Mainly floating and emergent types
- Observed primarily in Lakes/Ponds and Riverine wetlands
- **Baramulla:** 10,922 ha (post-monsoon)
- **Srinagar:** 6,254 ha (post-monsoon)
- **Budgam:** 1,927 ha (post-monsoon)

### Turbidity
- **General:** Low across most Valley wetlands in both seasons
- **Exceptions:**
  - Baramulla Lakes/Ponds: High turbidity in pre-monsoon (5,269 ha)
  - Srinagar: Low to Medium (post-monsoon), High to Low (pre-monsoon)

4. Encroachment

## Implementation Details

### Data Model Extensions

The `WaterEntity` interface has been extended with NWIA reference fields:

```typescript
export interface WaterEntity {
  // ... existing fields
  nwiaCode?: string; // NWIA wetland classification code (e.g., '1101', '1103')
  nwiaSignificance?: string; // Hydrological significance from NWIA Atlas
}
```

## Usage Guidelines

1. **Kashmir-Specific Only:** This source is used exclusively for Kashmir Valley wetland and hydrological intelligence. Do not generalize to other regions.

2. **Scientific Credibility:** NWIA data provides authoritative classification and area statistics. Use to enhance entity accuracy and scientific credibility.

3. **Visual Integration:** NWIA information is presented in compact, intelligent cards and badges - not as dry report content.

4. **District Linking:** All wetland entities are linked to their respective Kashmir districts as per NWIA classification.

5. **Classification Consistency:** NWIA codes (1101, 1103, 1104, 1106, 1202) are used consistently across the platform for wetland type identification.
